British Museum

The British Museum is the national museum of Britain. It was founded in 1753 by Sir Hans Sloane. The British Museum is a very popular place to visit, with so much to see and learn. It has many different collections, including Ancient Egypt, the Near East, Asia, Greece and Rome, Africa, European history, and science.

There is also a section with drawings and artworks by various famous artists. In the year 2000, Queen Elizabeth II officially opened the Great Court, a large covered square in the centre of the museum.

Tower of London

The tower of London is one of the most famous tourist attractions in the entire Britain. There are around 3 million visitors every year that visit the famous Tower of London. With over 1000 years of history, the tower was used for a royal palace, fortress, prison and even a place for execution.

The tower is located in east London.In the Tower of London you can see The Crown Jewels, the Traitor´s gate, Execution sites, The tower ravens, The white tower and the Londinium. I can promise you that this is a must-visit for history lovers and a great family experience.

Buckingham Palace

Of course, we can't forget about Buckingham Palace — the official residence of the British Royal Family. This iconic building has a rich and fascinating history. Although many people think of it as a castle, Buckingham Palace was originally built as a townhouse in 1703 for the Duke of Buckingham. It became a royal residence in 1837 when Queen Victoria moved in.

The palace has faced damage several times throughout history. One notable event occurred during the Second World War, on September 13, 1940, when it was bombed and partially destroyed. Despite this, the palace has been restored and remains a symbol of the British monarchy. Today, King Charles the third resides at Buckingham Palace. The building is enormous, with five floors. It includes 19 state rooms used for official events, 52 royal and guest bedrooms, 188 staff bedrooms, 92 offices, and 78 bathrooms.

Visitors can take guided tours of parts of the palace during certain times of the year. You can also witness the famous Changing of the Guard outside. And if you're lucky, you might even spot a member of the Royal Family!

Sea Life

The famous Sea Life Brighton is one of the oldest operating aquariums in the world, located in Brighton, England. A perfect place to see the beauty of the ocean with sharks, rays, turtles, seahorses and more!

The main attraction is the fascinating glass tunnel, where you can see all the beautiful sea creatures and learn about life in the ocean. You can also feed turtles, take the glass bottom boat and even try a Virtual Reality Experience with VR and headset for a whole new experience with today's technology. Sea Life also focuses on conservation and education, helping to protect the ocean and its creatures for future generations. It's a fun and educational day out!



Tower Brigde

Tower Bridge is one of the most iconic landmarks in all of Britain. During the London 2012 Olympic Games, the bridge was used as a symbolic centerpiece, proudly displaying the Olympic rings between its towers.

The bridge spans the River Thames with a total length of 244 meters and stands approximately 60 meters above sea level at the top of its towers. Between the towers, visitors can walk across a high-level glass-floored walkway, offering spectacular views of London.

Construction of Tower Bridge began in 1886 and was completed in 1894. It was officially opened by The Prince of Wales (later King Edward VII) and Princess Alexandra.

London Eye

Welcome to what was the tallest Ferris wheel in the world in the year 2000! Although it no longer holds that title, the London Eye remains one of the largest and most iconic observation wheels in the world.

Located on the South Bank of the River Thames, it has become a famous landmark of England. The wheel features 32 glass capsules, each capable of holding up to 25 people, allowing a maximum of 800 passengers at a time.

A full rotation takes approximately 30 minutes, It's a perfect place to take friends and loved ones to an unforgettable experience.
